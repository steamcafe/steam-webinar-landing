Webinar Info

Discovering the Nature of Science Using Magic Fish

1 What this webinar is about

Students conduct an investigation using “magic fish” to understand the nature of science. This activity is appropriate for K-6th grades. In the webinar, we will discuss how teachers can adapt the activity to varying skill levels.

2 What we’ll do

This webinar will briefly outline the three dimensions of the Next Generation Science Standards with an emphasis on the science and engineering practices. We will using small toy fish to experience the different stages of the 5E learning model before we discuss how to teach this activity in a classroom setting using an inquiry-based approach. During this time, I will share tips, tricks, pitfalls, and best practices used with this activity.

3 What else you’ll need to know

This is an active webinar where we ask participants to explore and discuss. Attendees who have signed up early to the webinar will receive a classroom set of fish in advance. During the webinar, we will use these fish to engage in group-based interactive activities using our web cams and microphones to discuss our observations, inferences, and applications to the classroom setting. You may want to take notes.

4 What you will receive:

  • A class set of materials;
  • A deeper understanding of how you can scaffold the science and engineering practices of the NGSS in your classroom using the 5E model;
  • An inquiry-based, student-centered, activity that you can immediately use in your classroom;
  • Ideas for how to extend this activity in a cross-curricular fashion to other subjects; and
  • Access to people who can give you support with this activity.

About The Host

Expert who will be conducting this webinar

Yen Verhoeven

CEO, Qi Learning Research Group

I am a former high school science teacher, college biotech and pharm tech department chair, learning theorist and an online instructional designer. When my boys were in elementary school, I worked with their teachers to develop interactive science activities to engage students in the doing of science. I found my passion in supporting teachers in the important work that they do by helping them connect learning theory to the practices of teaching. I’ve taught people of all ages and from all walks of life, and the favorite part of my job is when I get to see the glimmer in their eye when they get that “AH HA!” moment. That moment is when the person’s own intelligence gets to show itself, and the light of that person’s brilliance shines for everyone to see.
